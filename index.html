What’s Actually Going On

Three Patterns Keeping You Stuck

You can’t solve a problem you can’t see. These three patterns run beneath every decision you make — and they were installed long before you chose them.

01

The Roles You Play

The Strong One. The Fixer. The One Who Always Figures It Out. These survival identities earned you safety and praise. People began to admire you for the mask. Eventually, you forgot you were wearing it. Now the role runs you.

02

The Automatic Reactions

Anticipating conflict before it arrives. Over-explaining. Staying busy to avoid stillness. These reflexes once protected you. Now they run on autopilot — making decisions for you before you’re even aware there was a choice.

03

The Hidden Beliefs

"If I rest, I’m weak." "If I don’t prove my value, I won’t be loved." "If I slow down, I’ll fall behind." These unconscious rules were written long ago. They don’t announce themselves. They feel like personality. But they’re running the show.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is identity alignment coaching?

Identity alignment coaching helps successful professionals close the gap between who they’ve become internally and the role they’re still playing externally. Unlike traditional executive coaching that focuses on performance or strategy, identity alignment addresses the root cause of why high-achievers feel stuck, exhausted, or unfulfilled despite their success.

It works by identifying the unconscious patterns — outdated roles, automatic reactions, and hidden beliefs — that were installed early in life and no longer serve who you are today.

How do I know if I need a coach or a therapist?

Therapy is generally focused on healing past wounds, processing trauma, and addressing clinical mental health concerns. Coaching is forward-focused: it’s about closing the gap between where you are and where you want to be.

Many of Martin’s clients have done therapy and found it helpful — but they hit a ceiling. They don’t need to process more of the past. They need to stop operating from an identity they’ve outgrown. If you’re high-functioning but feel like you’re performing a version of yourself that no longer fits, coaching is likely the right next step.

What’s the difference between this and executive coaching?

Most executive coaching focuses on leadership skills, communication, and performance optimization. Martin’s work goes deeper. Instead of adding more tools to your toolkit, he helps you identify and release the unconscious patterns that are running your life.

The result isn’t just better performance. It’s a fundamental shift in how you experience your success, your relationships, and yourself.

Why do successful people feel unfulfilled?

Success often comes from an identity that was built for survival — being the strong one, the provider, the person who always figures it out. These identities work brilliantly for building careers and earning respect. But over time, you outgrow them.

The strategies that got you here start suffocating you. You feel exhausted not because of what you do, but because of who you have to be to do it. This isn’t burnout and it’s not a midlife crisis. It’s a misalignment between who you’ve become and the role you’re still playing.

What happens in a discovery session?

The discovery session is a free, 2-hour deep conversation — not a sales pitch. Martin maps your specific patterns: the roles you play, the automatic reactions that run your decisions, and the hidden beliefs that keep you stuck.

Most clients say it’s the most honest conversation they’ve had in years. By the end, you’ll have a clear picture of where the misalignment lives in your life and what it would take to close the gap.

It knows your story, understands your specific challenges, and uses Martin’s coaching methodology calibrated to you.

It provides daily check-ins, guided exercises, and on-demand coaching support 24/7. Think of it as having Martin’s framework available whenever you need it, personalized to your life.

How is this different from self-help books and programs?

Self-help gives you information. Coaching gives you transformation. The difference is specificity: books speak to everyone, coaching speaks to you.

Most self-help addresses symptoms — better habits, more discipline, improved time management. Martin’s work addresses the invisible operating system beneath those symptoms: the identity patterns that determine how you show up in every area of your life. You can’t read your way out of a pattern you can’t see.

Who is this coaching for?

Martin works with high-achieving professionals — executives, founders, and leaders — who have built successful lives but feel a growing disconnect between their external success and their internal experience.

His clients are typically earning well, respected in their field, and performing at a high level. But privately, they feel exhausted by the role they’re playing, their relationships are suffering, or they’ve hit an invisible wall that no amount of strategy can break through.

How long does coaching take to see results?

Most clients experience a significant shift within the first discovery session — a clarity about what’s been driving their behavior that they couldn’t see before. The 1:1 coaching engagement is 12 months because lasting transformation requires sustained work.

The first few months focus on identifying and releasing old patterns. The middle months are about building new ways of operating. The final months are about making alignment sustainable. The AI coach option provides ongoing support for those who want to work at their own pace.

Can coaching help my marriage or relationships?

Yes — and this is one of the most common outcomes. When you’re operating from an outdated identity, your closest relationships bear the heaviest cost. The people who love you can feel that something is off, even if they can’t name it.

As you realign with who you actually are, your relationships transform — not because your partner changes, but because you show up differently. Martin’s own story includes saving his marriage and rebuilding his relationship with his son through this exact process.
